Jeremiah  45 : 1 - 5

45 This is the word that Jeremiah the prophet spoke to Bar′uch the son of Ne·ri′ah when he wrote in a book these words dictated by Jeremiah in the fourth year of Je·hoi′a·kim son of Jo·si′ah, the king of Judah:

2 “This is what Jehovah the God of Israel says concerning you, Bar′uch, 3 ‘You have said: “Woe to me, for Jehovah has added grief to my pain! I am weary from my groaning, and I have found no resting-place.”’

4 “You should say to him, ‘This is what Jehovah says: “Look! What I have built up I am tearing down, and what I have planted I am uprooting—the entire land. 5 But you are seeking* great things for yourself. Stop seeking such things.”’

“‘For I am about to bring a calamity on all flesh,’* declares Jehovah, ‘and wherever you may go, I will grant you your life* as a spoil.’”*

^ Jer. 45:5 Or “expecting.”

^ Jer. 45:5 Or “people.”

^ Jer. 45:5 Or “soul.”

^ Jer. 45:5 Or “will let you escape with your life.”
